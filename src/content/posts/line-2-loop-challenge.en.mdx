Seoul Line 2 is a favorite long challenge because it is a loop with many familiar names and enough length to punish early typos. Community players often warm up on short stretches, then use Line 1 or Line 2 when they want an endurance record.

## Do not open with the full loop

Start with a short segment on Line 2 (or another familiar line) until your hands settle. Jumping straight into the whole circle turns one early typo into a demoralizing stop-and-go commute.

## Watch transfer clusters

Transfer-heavy areas and similar station names are where runs break. Preview the next station while finishing the current one. If the same cluster fails twice, drill only that stretch before another full attempt. More on this in [tips](/tips).

## Fix direction and options

Inner vs outer (or your chosen direction) must stay constant when comparing times. If you enable circular or unlimited options in the original game, understand how the sequence continues before you treat the finish time as a personal best.

## After the loop

Note the three stickiest stations, then run a short section that includes them. The next full loop usually improves more from that drill than from another all-out rush.
