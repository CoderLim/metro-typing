Route quiz is the memory-first mode in Metro Typing. Instead of racing an entire typing run, you guess station names hidden on the map. It pairs well with typing practice when you know a line visually but freeze on the spelling.

## What you can adjust

The original game lets you tune difficulty, question count, region or route filters, and whether transfer stations appear. On easier levels, **initial-consonant (초성) hints** may help. Harder settings usually drop those hints so recall does more of the work.

## How to practice with quiz

1. Pick a line you already type somewhat well.
2. Start with hints on, then turn them off once answers feel cheap.
3. Note which transfer clusters you miss — those are the same places that stop the train in typing mode.
4. Switch back to a short typing run on that stretch the same day.

## Quiz vs single play

Quiz builds the mental map. Single play builds the finger path. Alternating both on one line is more efficient than only chasing finish times.
