Metro Typing rewards rhythm more than raw speed. A clean run usually comes from typing station names in steady bursts, not from rushing every syllable block.

## Start With Accuracy

One typo can cost more time than a slightly slower word. Before trying to beat a record, play one round where the only goal is to avoid stops. Watch which station names make your hands hesitate, then repeat that same line.

## Keep the Next Station in Mind

Subway typing is partly memory work. Say the next station silently before you type it. This gives your hands a target and reduces the chance of starting with the wrong consonant or vowel.

## Use Short Practice Loops

Long lines are satisfying, but short repeated loops are better for fixing mistakes. Choose a familiar line, keep the same direction, and play two or three rounds. If the same station slows you down each time, practice that name separately before another full run.

## Check Your Input Setup

Hangul input mode, autocorrect, and keyboard layout matter. Before a timed round, confirm that your Korean input is active and that browser autocomplete is not fighting your typing flow.

The best Metro Typing records usually come from fewer stops, not frantic typing. Build accuracy first, then let speed follow.
