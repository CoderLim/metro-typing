Your first Metro Typing line should teach the loop — pick, type, review — without turning into an endurance test. A long circular route looks impressive, but it hides where you actually struggle.

## Prefer a line you already ride

Commute memory is free practice data. Home station, office station, and one or two transfer anchors give your hands something to prepare before each name appears. If you do not live in Seoul, pick any short stretch you can finish twice in a row without rage-quitting.

## Short section before full line

When the game lets you set departure and arrival, start narrow. A short section means fewer sticky stations and cleaner feedback on the result screen. After two clean finishes, widen the interval or switch to a longer route.

## One direction only

Upbound and downbound reshuffle the order. For the first week, keep one direction so finish times are comparable. Switching sides too early makes every “new personal best” hard to trust.

## When to move up

Move to longer challenges such as Line 1 or the Line 2 loop only after the warm-up stretch feels automatic. That is when [tips for sticky stations](/tips) matter more than raw line length.
