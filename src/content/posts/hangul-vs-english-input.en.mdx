Metro Typing can accept station names in Korean or English depending on the mode you select in the original game. Mixing both in one rushed session usually hurts more than it helps.

## Practice Hangul when…

- You want real Seoul commute spelling under your fingers
- You are warming up for Korean typing work or study
- You already know the line order and need finger accuracy

Keep autocomplete under control. Wrong suggestions break rhythm faster than a slow correct keystroke. See [tips](/tips) for input hygiene.

## Practice English when…

- You are still learning Hangul and want the map feedback without IME friction
- You want to compare romanized names you see on tourist maps
- You are drilling one line’s English spellings before switching back to Hangul later

## Keep one session consistent

Pick one input style for a full set of runs. Switching mid-line forces two muscle maps at once. After a clean Hangul week on a short stretch, try the same stretch in English (or the reverse) as a deliberate second skill — not as a panic fallback when you mistype.
