## Real maps, real station order

Metro Typing builds rounds from real railway networks. You pick a region and route, then type station names in order so the train advances on the map. Coverage is broad, but the exact list can change when the original game updates.

This page summarizes the publicly described coverage so you can choose a practice target. Always confirm the live line list inside the game on the [homepage](/#play).

## Capital region (Seoul metro area)

Typical coverage includes:

- Seoul Metro **Lines 1–9**
- Incheon metro and wider capital-area suburban lines
- Light rail lines in the region
- **GTX-A** and other metropolitan additions as the game lists them

This is the densest playground for most players: familiar commute lines, frequent transfers, and both short and very long stretches.

## Other Korean cities

Beyond Seoul, the original game has also listed urban rail in:

- **Busan** (metro lines, Donghae Line, Busan–Gimhae Light Rail, and related services as shown in-game)
- **Daegu** (metro lines and related regional links)
- **Gwangju** and **Daejeon** metro lines

These are useful when you want a change of spelling patterns and station-name length without leaving the same typing loop.

## Intercity and high-speed rail

Coverage may also include national services such as **KTX**, **SRT**, **ITX**, and **Mugunghwa** (and similar services listed in the selector). These runs feel different from urban metro: longer name stretches, fewer “every-day commute” anchors, and a good “change of flavor” practice set.

## How to choose a line

**If you are new**

- Pick a **short section** or a line you already ride
- Prefer one direction you know from commuting
- Finish two clean runs before chasing a long loop

**If you want a record challenge**

- Move to longer routes such as **Line 1** or the **Line 2** loop after your warm-up
- Keep the same departure/arrival and direction when comparing times
- Use express or circular options only when you understand how they change the station sequence

**If memory is the goal**

- Try [route quiz mode](/how-to-play) on a familiar line
- Drill transfer clusters that always slow you down in typing mode

## Coverage can change

Station lists, new openings, and option toggles follow the original service. If a line appears or disappears in the selector, trust the live game UI. This guide site does not operate the railway data and is **not** an official transit timetable.
