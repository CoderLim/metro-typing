## Is Metro Typing free?

Yes. You can play in the browser without paying. Pick a line and start. The original service has also described its modes as free, without ads or in-app purchases on the official app listings — confirm live details on the store pages if you use an app.

## Can I play without signing up?

Yes for basic **single play** and **route quiz**. Choose a line and type. Setup stays light.

Login is typically needed for **multiplayer rooms**, **profile**, and **official ranking** registration on the original game.

## Can I play on my phone?

The game opens in mobile browsers. Short practice stretches work. For fast station names, a physical keyboard is usually more comfortable.

Official mobile apps have also been released for Android and iOS by the original developer. This site still focuses on the browser play frame on the [homepage](/#play).

## How is the record calculated?

You usually see **finish time**, **typing speed**, and **accuracy** together. More typos mean more stops and a longer total time. Repeating the **same line and direction** makes progress easy to compare.

Official ranking compares route records using completion time, average keystrokes (or similar speed metrics), and accuracy, and may include anti-macro checks.

## Can I play if my Hangul typing is slow?

Yes. Start with a familiar short line to build accuracy, then try longer stretches. Learning the finish flow matters before raw speed. See [tips](/tips) for a practice rhythm.

## Do I need an account for multiplayer?

Multiplayer (up to four players on the same route, public/private rooms, invites) generally requires login on the original service. Agree on one line before racing so results are comparable. Details are in [how to play](/how-to-play).

## Is there a desktop or mobile app?

Yes — the original developer has published apps (for example Android, iOS/iPadOS, and Windows store listings). This website is an independent guide and browser entry point; app installs and accounts follow the original publisher.

## Is this an official railway service?

No. Metro Typing (the game) is a typing game that uses railway maps and station names. It is **not** a railway operator product and does not provide real train schedules.

This website (metrotyping.org) is an **independent** introduction and play page. The homepage frame loads the original game from [metrotyping.kr](https://metrotyping.kr/). Copyright and availability follow that service.
