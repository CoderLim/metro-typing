## Start a run

Metro Typing moves a train along a real railway map when you type each station name correctly. The playable frame on the [homepage](/#play) loads the original browser game. This page explains the flow in more detail so you can choose a mode and settings with less guesswork.

1. Open the game on the homepage (or the original site) and pick a **region**.
2. Choose a **route**, then set a **departure** and **arrival** station when the mode asks for them.
3. Confirm direction or interval options (upbound/downbound, express sections, and similar toggles where available).
4. Type the station name shown in the center of the screen. A correct entry advances the train; a typo keeps you at the same stop until you fix it.
5. Finish the stretch to review **time**, **typing speed**, and **accuracy**, then replay the same route to compare.

You can often choose whether the train advances **automatically** after a correct name or only after **Space / Enter**. Automatic advance feels faster once you trust your accuracy; confirm-to-advance helps when you want a short pause between stops.

## Single play

Single play is the default solo challenge. Pick the area, line, and section you want, then chase a personal record.

- Use a **short section** to warm up your hands.
- Use a **long route** (for example Seoul Lines 1 or 2) when you want an endurance record.
- After a run, note which stations broke your rhythm and drill that stretch again.

Single play and route quiz can usually be used **without logging in**. Login becomes more important when you want a profile, multiplayer rooms, or official ranking submission.

## Real-time multiplayer

Multiplayer lets up to **four players** start the same route together and race finish times. Rooms can be public or private, and friend invites are supported in the original game.

Expect to **sign in** for multiplayer and ranking features. Agree on one line and direction before the race so everyone’s result is comparable.

## Route quiz

Route quiz hides station names on the map and asks you to guess them. You can adjust difficulty, question count, region/route filters, and whether transfer stations appear.

On easier difficulties, **initial-consonant (초성) hints** may appear. On hard difficulty, those hints are typically off, so memory carries more of the load. Quiz mode is useful when you want line knowledge without a full end-to-end typing run.

## Official ranking

Route records are compared using completion time, average keystrokes (or similar speed metrics), and accuracy. Single play and quiz work without an account, but **registering an official ranking** usually needs a profile. Ranking matches may include anti-macro checks on the original service.

Treat ranking as optional. Most practice value comes from repeating the same line locally and watching your own typos fall.

## Useful settings

Depending on the route, the original game may offer:

- **Express** sections where available
- **Unlimited round-trip / circular** runs for loops
- **Transfer-route** options
- **Korean or English** station-name input

Supported lines and options change as the original service updates. If a toggle is missing for a line, it is usually because that option does not apply to the route — not because this guide site is incomplete.

## Practice rhythm that works

Community players often:

- Warm up on a short familiar stretch, then attempt a long line
- Say or preview the **next** station while finishing the current one
- Keep one line and one direction fixed when comparing records

Mistakes feel costly because the train stops until the name is correct. Accuracy first, then speed.
